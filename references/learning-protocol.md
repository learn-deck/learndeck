# Learning protocol and why it is effective

PatchQuest uses retrieval, short practice, feedback, and revisits rather than
long passive chapters. The protocol is evidence-informed, but it makes no
claim that one method, interval, or score works identically for every learner.

## The loop

1. **Diagnose from memory.** Ask one low-stakes question before reopening the
   source. Record confidence separately from correctness.
2. **Acquire one idea.** Read the smallest relevant source, trace, or worked
   example—not an entire topic dump.
3. **Act in the workspace.** Create or modify one named code path, run one
   bounded command, and keep the evidence local to that path.
4. **Explain and self-check.** The learner explains the decision and names the
   evidence before seeing the evaluation.
5. **Evaluate and revise.** The agent compares the answer with a named source,
   identifies one exact gap, and asks for a separate revision when needed.
6. **Revisit a related distinction.** Store a review question in the
   per-workspace database. On a later session, retrieve before rereading.

## Why these choices are here

| Choice | Why it helps | Important limitation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Retrieval before rereading | Attempting to recall reveals what is not yet available and can improve later retention relative to restudy alone. | Beginners need an initial explanation or cue; a blank test is not always useful. |
| A later related question | Spacing a revisit reduces dependence on short-term familiarity. | Exact gaps should depend on the learner's result and goal, not a universal schedule. |
| Worked example → partial cue → independent work | Guidance lowers needless search while the pattern is new, then fades as knowledge grows. | Guidance can become redundant; do not keep scaffolds after they stop helping. |
| Explanation plus criterion-based feedback | Explaining exposes causal gaps; specific feedback gives a usable correction. | A fluent answer is not automatically correct, and an automated record cannot semantically grade prose. |
| Related contrasts | Comparing genuinely confusable concepts helps choose the right rule in context. | Do not mix unrelated material merely to make practice harder. |
