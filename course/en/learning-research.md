# Research basis for the learning protocol

PatchQuest treats these findings as design constraints, not universal laws or
promises about an individual learner. The protocol combines source-closed
retrieval after acquisition, adaptive spacing, related-concept interleaving,
fading worked examples, checked self-explanation, and informative corrective
feedback. Support depends on prior knowledge and observed evidence.

## Research anchors

| Protocol choice                      | Evidence anchor                                                                                                                                      | Qualification used here                                                                                    |
| ------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Retrieve rather than only reread     | [Roediger & Karpicke (2006)](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1467-9280.2006.01693.x); [Rowland (2014)](https://doi.org/10.1037/a0037559)                   | Retrieval follows acquisition; novices may use cues or a worked example.                                   |
| Revisit after a gap                  | [Cepeda et al. (2006)](https://doi.org/10.1037/0033-2909.132.3.354); [Carpenter, Pan & Butler (2022)](https://doi.org/10.1038/s44159-022-00089-1)    | The interval is provisional and depends on goal and performance.                                           |
| Mix related, confusable cases        | [Rohrer, Dedrick & Stershic (2015)](https://doi.org/10.1037/edu0000001); [Brunmair & Richter (2019)](https://doi.org/10.1037/bul0000209)             | Interleaving begins after initial fluency and never mixes unrelated topics for difficulty alone.           |
| Fade guidance with growing knowledge | [Sweller & Cooper (1985)](https://doi.org/10.1207/s1532690xci0201_3); [Kalyuga et al. (2003)](https://doi.org/10.1207/S15326985EP3801_4)             | Worked examples progress to explanation, partial completion, independent work, then transfer.              |
| Check self-explanations              | [Chi et al. (1989)](https://doi.org/10.1207/s15516709cog1302_1); [Bisra et al. (2018)](https://doi.org/10.1007/s10648-018-9434-x)                    | Explanations are compared with repository evidence so fluency cannot preserve an error.                    |
| Prefer diagnostic feedback           | [Hattie & Timperley (2007)](https://doi.org/10.3102/003465430298487); [Wisniewski, Zierer & Hattie (2020)](https://doi.org/10.3389/fpsyg.2019.03087) | Feedback identifies target, evidence, gap, correction, process, next action, and self-monitoring question. |

The protocol deliberately makes no claim that one interval, question format,
working-memory number, feedback timing, or effect-size multiplier applies to
everyone. Sleep, health, and attention matter to learning, but PatchQuest does
not encode speculative neurobiological mechanisms into its completion rules.

Its automated validator is deliberately structural. It can prove that a
diagnostic preceded active work, checks actually ran, mastery followed proof,
revisions and delayed reviews are linked, and evidence was not retroactively
changed. It cannot infer that a learner's prose is true or that feedback caused
learning. The guide must evaluate open answers against repository sources, and
the learner must preserve criterion-specific self-evidence for later review.
